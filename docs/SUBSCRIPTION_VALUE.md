# How to Increase Subscription Value for Trivense

This document outlines strategies to increase the perceived value of Trivense’s subscription so users are willing to pay more and stay longer.

---

## 1. Define Clear Tiers and Value

### Free vs Paid (today → target)

| Free tier | Paid tier (Premium) |
|-----------|----------------------|
| 1–2 boards | Unlimited boards |
| Limited history (e.g. 3 months) | Full history + export |
| Basic categories | Custom categories + rules |
| Ads or branding | No ads, optional white-label |
| 1 shared board | Unlimited shared boards + roles |
| — | Advanced analytics & insights |
| — | Recurring expenses & reminders |
| — | Priority support / early features |

**Action:** In the app and on the website, show a simple comparison table: “Free vs Premium” so the upgrade path is obvious.

---

## 2. Features That Increase Willingness to Pay

### High impact (do first)

1. **Recurring expenses & reminders**  
   Subscriptions, rent, EMI. “Your Netflix charge is due” → fewer missed entries and more accurate budgets. Strong retention driver.

2. **Better analytics & insights**  
   - Spending by category (with trends).  
   - “You spend 40% more on weekends.”  
   - Budget vs actual charts, monthly/yearly reports.  
   - Export to PDF/Excel for premium only.

3. **Smarter sharing & splits**  
   - “Who owes whom” with one-tap “I paid” / “Settle.”  
   - Optional payment links (UPI/PayPal).  
   - Roles: Admin vs Member (who can add/delete).  
   - Activity log: “X added ₹500 – Dinner.”

4. **Backup, sync & export (premium)**  
   - Auto backup to Google Drive / iCloud (premium).  
   - Export all data (CSV/PDF) for premium.  
   - Clear “Restore from backup” flow.

### Medium impact

5. **Bank / card sync (read-only)**  
   Link accounts, auto-import transactions, auto-categorize. Big perceived value; needs compliance and partner APIs.

6. **Goals & buckets**  
   “Trip fund,” “Emergency fund” with target amount and progress. Connects saving to your data.

7. **Receipts & attachments**  
   Photo per expense, optional OCR for amount/date. Reduces manual entry and improves trust in numbers.

8. **Multi-currency & travel**  
   Trip mode: one board, multiple currencies, conversion on add. Good for your “share with companions” positioning.

### Nice to have

9. **Recurring budgets**  
   Monthly/weekly budget that resets; alerts when approaching limit.

10. **Templates**  
    “Monthly template” (rent, groceries, etc.) to quick-fill a new month.

11. **Family / household plan**  
    One subscription, multiple accounts (e.g. 2–5 members). Increases ARPU and retention for shared boards.

---

## 3. Pricing & Packaging

### Principles

- **Anchor with annual:** Show “₹X/month, billed annually” and “2 months free” so monthly feels cheaper.
- **One main paid plan first:** Avoid too many choices. E.g. one “Premium” plan, later add “Pro” or “Family.”
- **Local pricing:** INR primary; USD/other for expansion. Consider ₹99–199/month or ₹999–1999/year.
- **Trial:** 7–14 day free trial of Premium (no card, or card with clear “cancel anytime”) to reduce friction.

### Example structure

| Plan    | Price (example) | Best for           |
|---------|------------------|--------------------|
| Free    | ₹0               | Try app, 1–2 boards |
| Premium | ₹99/mo or ₹999/yr | Individuals & small groups |
| Family  | ₹199/mo or ₹1999/yr | 2–5 members, shared boards |

---

## 4. Increase Perceived Value (Without Always Building More)

- **Onboarding:** Short “value tour” after signup: “Create a board → Add an expense → Invite a friend.” Show outcome: “You’ll know who owes what.”
- **In-app wins:** After first shared board or first month of data: “You tracked ₹X this month” + “See trends in Premium.”
- **Email / push:** Weekly or monthly digest: “You spent ₹X; top category: Food.” Link “See full insights” to premium.
- **Social proof:** “Join 50,000+ users” / “Rated 4.6 on Play Store” on paywall and landing page.
- **Guarantee:** “Cancel anytime” or “7-day money-back” to reduce perceived risk.

---

## 5. Reduce Churn (Retention = Value)

- **Exit survey:** On cancel, ask “Why?” (price, missing feature, not using). Use to prioritize.
- **Pause instead of cancel:** “Pause for 1–3 months” keeps them in ecosystem and can bring them back.
- **Win-back offers:** After churn, email after 2–4 weeks: “We added [feature]. Come back at 50% off for 3 months.”
- **Usage nudges:** If no login for 14 days: “Your last expense was ₹X. Add today’s?” Re-engagement keeps subscription valuable.

---

## 6. Differentiation (Why Trivense Over Others)

- **Positioning:** “Expense tracking that’s built for sharing” (trips, roommates, events). Double down on shared boards and splits.
- **Local-first:** Language (e.g. Hindi), UPI, local categories, INR and Indian tax/export if needed.
- **Simplicity:** Fewer taps to add an expense and to see “who owes whom” than competitors.
- **Trust:** Clear privacy policy, “your data is yours,” export and delete. Helps for family/shared use.

Call this out on the paywall: “Trivense is made for shared expenses and trips.”

---

## 7. Quick-Win Roadmap

| Phase | Focus | Outcome |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Now** | Define Free vs Premium limits in app + paywall screen | Clear reason to upgrade |
| **Short (1–2 months)** | Recurring expenses, better analytics screen, export (PDF/CSV) for Premium | More “power user” value |
| **Medium (2–4 months)** | “Who owes whom” + settle flow, backup to Drive, receipt photo | Stronger sharing + trust |
| **Longer** | Bank sync (if feasible), Family plan, goals/buckets | Higher ARPU and retention |

---

## 8. Metrics to Watch

- **Conversion:** Free → trial → paid (%).
- **ARPU:** Revenue / paying users.
- **Churn:** Monthly and annual; by cohort (signup month).
- **LTV:** Average revenue per user over lifetime; compare Free vs Premium.
- **Feature usage:** Which features do paying users use most? Double down there in messaging and development.

---

## Summary

To increase subscription value:

1. **Make the paid tier obviously better:** Clear limits on Free, clear benefits on Premium.  
2. **Add high-impact features:** Recurring expenses, insights, export, better sharing/splits.  
3. **Price simply:** One main Premium plan, annual discount, trial.  
4. **Show value often:** Onboarding, digests, in-app nudges.  
5. **Reduce churn:** Pause, win-back, exit surveys.  
6. **Differentiate:** “Built for shared expenses and trips” + local experience.

Use this doc as a living plan: update tiers, pricing, and roadmap as you ship and learn from users.
